This study extends recent work in the climate literature by examining how climate shocks- specifically temperature and precipitation anomaliesâ€” spill over across borders, influencing inflation and real economic variables. Using high-resolution gridded climate data, I construct sector-sensitive climate shock measures and embed them in a large two-country VAR framework for Italy and Malta. The analysis shows that climate shocks in Italy can generate inflationary pressures in Malta, particularly through processed food prices, services, and producer prices in food manufacturing.
